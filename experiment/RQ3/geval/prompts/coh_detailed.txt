You will be given SRS files through file input.

Your task is to rate the CandidateSRS on one metric only: Cohesiveness.

Attached files:
- CandidateSRS: the generated SRS to evaluate.
- ReferenceExample: a high-quality reference SRS for the same problem space, when available.


Evaluation Criteria:

Cohesiveness (1-5) - whether the CandidateSRS reads as a unified, coherent, problem-specific specification.

Assess the CandidateSRS primarily on its own internal structure and consistency. Use ReferenceExample only as a loose qualitative benchmark for how concrete and problem-specific a good SRS can be. ReferenceExample is not a required template, and the CandidateSRS should not be penalized for using different sections, wording, or ordering.

Focus on:
- logical structure and flow
- consistent terminology
- coherent workflows and states
- consistent roles, responsibilities, interfaces, and requirement boundaries
- absence of major internal contradictions

Do not deeply re-score missing coverage or external truth for this metric. Missing requirements belong to Completeness. Unsupported or false external claims belong to Correctness.

A document should not receive a high cohesiveness score merely because it is neatly formatted. If it compresses important domain roles, workflows, interfaces, and states into a generic menu/order/admin description, its cohesiveness should be lower because it does not cohere as a problem-specific SRS.


Evaluation Steps:

1. Read CandidateSRS carefully.
2. Assess whether its sections, terminology, roles, workflows, states, interfaces, and requirements form one consistent specification.
3. Use ReferenceExample only as a loose qualitative benchmark, not as a mandatory structure.
4. Assign a score from 1 to 5 for cohesiveness only.
