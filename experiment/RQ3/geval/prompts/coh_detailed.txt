Evaluation Criteria:

Cohesiveness (1-5) - whether the CandidateSRS reads as a unified, coherent, problem-specific specification. Assess the CandidateSRS primarily on its own internal structure and consistency. Use ReferenceExample only as a loose qualitative benchmark for how concrete and problem-specific a good SRS can be. ReferenceExample is not a required template, and the CandidateSRS should not be penalized for using different sections, wording, or ordering.


Score Scale:

5 = Excellent: unified, coherent, problem-specific, and internally consistent.
4 = Good: mostly coherent with only minor structural or terminology inconsistencies.
3 = Fair: understandable but has noticeable fragmentation, generic sections, or inconsistent terminology.
2 = Weak: poorly organized or frequently inconsistent across sections, roles, workflows, or requirements.
1 = Poor: incoherent, generic, or not readable as one unified specification.


Evaluation Steps:

1. Read CandidateSRS carefully.
2. Assess whether its sections, terminology, roles, workflows, states, interfaces, and requirements form one consistent specification.
3. Use ReferenceExample only as a loose qualitative benchmark, not as a mandatory structure.
4. Assign a score from 1 to 5 for cohesiveness only.
