You will be given SRS files through file input.

Your task is to rate the CandidateSRS on one metric only: Completeness.

Attached files:
- CandidateSRS: the generated SRS to evaluate.
- ReferenceExample: a high-quality reference SRS for the same problem space, when available.


Evaluation Criteria:

Completeness (1-5) - semantic coverage of the important requirement intents for this target system. Use ReferenceExample as a coverage boundary, not as a required template. The CandidateSRS does not need to match the reference wording, section layout, order, or document length. It should receive credit when it covers the same important requirement intent in its own words.

System modeling sections in ReferenceExample may be used to understand requirement intents, actors, workflows, states, and data concepts. CandidateSRS should not be penalized solely for lacking diagrams, image assets, or identical modeling sections unless those omissions also remove important requirement intent.

Completeness should reward concrete, validation-ready coverage, not only high-level topic coverage. A generic SRS that covers only the main happy-path actor functions but omits important exception flows, operational controls, traceable details, and measurable non-functional constraints should receive at most 3.

When judging coverage, pay special attention to whether CandidateSRS covers important role-specific workflows and operational details such as order cancellation/refund handling, customer support or dispute handling, payment transaction records or reconciliation, promotion/coupon rules, restaurant availability/stock/business-hour handling, delivery assignment/rejection/reassignment/proof of delivery, admin RBAC/audit/monitoring, privacy/security/compliance requirements, and measurable non-functional targets.

Additional requirements are acceptable when they are consistent with the system boundary and represent reasonable elaboration for the same problem domain. Do not require CandidateSRS to copy ReferenceExample exactly.


Score Scale:

5 = Excellent: covers nearly all important requirement intents, including core workflows, role-specific operations, important exception flows, operational controls, and measurable non-functional constraints.
4 = Good: covers most core and role-specific workflows with only minor omissions in exception flows, operational details, or measurable non-functional constraints.
3 = Fair: covers major happy-path workflows for most actors, but misses several important exception flows, operational controls, traceable details, or measurable non-functional constraints.
2 = Weak: covers only a subset of core workflows or describes them mostly at a high level, with many important actor responsibilities, exception flows, operational controls, or quality constraints missing.
1 = Poor: fails to cover the target system's core requirement intents.


Evaluation Steps:

1. Read CandidateSRS and ReferenceExample carefully.
2. Identify the important requirement intents in ReferenceExample, treating them as semantic coverage boundaries rather than wording or structure requirements.
3. Check which important intents are adequately covered by CandidateSRS.
4. Assign a score from 1 to 5 for completeness only.
